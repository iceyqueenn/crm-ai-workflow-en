Benefits & Risks

✦
Key Benefits
  • Dramatically faster delivery — a working product connected to live data in weeks, not months
  • Overnight async work — AI doesn't sleep; results are ready to consume every morning
  • Cross-role coding — PM, Design, and Eng all write code, massively shortening the handoff chain
  • More realistic prototypes — live data prototypes surface issues that static Figma mocks never could
  • Faster traction validation — dogfooding stage reveals product-market fit without waiting for a full build cycle
  • Sprint waste eliminated — AI replaces large portions of UXR sprint work; no more multi-week ideation cycles
⚠
Limitations & Risks
  • Cursor prototypes don't connect to live data; poor collaborative visibility compared to Figma's shared canvas
  • High conversion cost from Cursor → Figma; no tool currently bridges the two cleanly
  • Requires significant upfront investment to build Component Libraries and Cursor Templates before vibe coding is reliable
  • Tool access barriers in China (Claude Code / Cursor unavailable) — alternative solutions needed
  • UI quality bar is intentionally lowered in early stages — scrappy output is rough by design
  • No single role can fully replace the team yet — PM + Design + Eng collaboration remains essential for now
Core Insight · Meta

It used to take weeks of sprint work — concept designs, reviews, PRD writing — before an engineer could write a single line of code.
Now it's completely inverted: AI explores ideas overnight, the team filters in the morning, and within days there's a playable prototype connected to real data. The quality of product conversation that becomes possible is in an entirely different league.
